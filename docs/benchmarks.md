# Benchmarks — protocol and reproducibility

This document describes the exact protocol behind each number reported in the README. Every benchmark is regenerated by `orcadolittle benchmark` or `python -m orcadolittle.benchmarks.run_all`, with a deterministic seed (default 42).

## Perception

* **Dataset.** DCLDE 2026 (Palmer 2025), held-out 20 % split, seed 42.
* **Backbone.** AVES2 (`EarthSpeciesProject/esp-aves2-sl-beats-bio`) and Perch 2.0 in two configurations: frozen and linear-probe.
* **Heads.** Ecotype: 3-way logistic regression with class-balanced weights. Call type: per-ecotype multinomial regression over the dialect vocabulary in `orcadolittle.data.context_mapping`.
* **Metrics.** Ecotype accuracy (macro-averaged across the three classes), call-type top-1 and top-5 accuracy (macro-averaged across the dialect vocabulary), per-ecotype confusion matrices, calibration (Brier score).
* **Baselines.** Palmer 2025 baseline ecotype classifier (~0.93 accuracy on similar splits); Bergler et al. 2019 ORCA-SPOT detector for cross-check on detection precision.

## Generation

* **Reference distribution.** DCLDE 2026 held-out audio re-encoded through the perception encoder.
* **Synthesised distribution.** *n* synthesised clips per (ecotype × call type) cell, *n* configurable (`generate_eval.run(n_synth=...)`).
* **Distributional distance.** KID (kernel inception distance) using the perception encoder as the feature extractor. Lower is better.
* **Conditional fidelity.** A separately trained ecotype classifier scored on the synthesised audio; we report top-1 accuracy as the recovery rate.
* **Repertoire-gate pass rate.** Fraction of synthesised clips that pass the per-ecotype Mahalanobis check before broadcast.

## Selection policy (off-policy)

* **Logged dataset.** The published playback corpus in `orcadolittle/data/playback_corpus.py` (~138 trials, target 300–500 in v0.2).
* **Estimator.** Doubly-robust importance-weighted policy value (DR-IPS). The behaviour policy is the empirical action distribution implied by the experimenters' choice. The target policy is one of `thompson`, `greedy`, `uniform`, `abstain`.
* **Baselines.** Uniform-random selection (value 0 by definition), greedy-mean selection.
* **Confidence intervals.** 95 % CIs by 1 000-sample bootstrap over per-trial weights.

## Response predictor

* **Training.** Same playback corpus, per-condition supervision, leave-one-paper-out cross-validation to control for paper-level confounders.
* **Metrics.** Macro-F1 across `reply` / `approach` / `avoid` / `no_response`; per-class F1; calibration (reliability curve, Brier).
* **Cross-species transfer.** Pretrain on bottlenose-dolphin playback corpus; evaluate transfer macro-F1 on the orca subset. Treated as an upper bound on what the cross-species transfer prior gives us when orca data is thin.
* **Falsifiability check.** Per-trial total-variation distance between the real-stimulus prediction and the closer of the two counterfactual predictions; the report classifies trials into the IPCC tiers.

## Closed loop

* **Latency.** End-to-end wall-clock from hydrophone-frame in to broadcast-waveform out, on a single A100 (target < 200 ms per pass).
* **Throughput.** Sustained passes per second on a 5-minute Salish Sea clip.
* **Robustness.** Mean ± SD of per-call decisions across 10 seeds; we expect the policy choice to be stable in the high-confidence regime.

## Hardware

Reference numbers are produced on:
* 1 × NVIDIA A100 40 GB (training-side).
* 1 × NVIDIA RTX 3060 12 GB (inference-side, also the Docker target).
* CPU-only fallback for the perception + selection paths (no generation).

The Docker image (`orcadolittle/docker/Dockerfile`) reproduces the inference numbers verbatim on either GPU.

## Determinism

Every benchmark sets:
* `numpy` and `torch` seeds to 42 (override via `--seed`).
* `PYTHONHASHSEED=42`.
* `torch.backends.cudnn.deterministic=True` when CUDA is used.

Per-benchmark JSON outputs and per-run hashes are written under `runs/<git-sha>/` so the audit trail survives across machines.
