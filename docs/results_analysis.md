# Results Analysis

Run `20260529_072930` encoded 27,934 DCLDE call-level segments (768-dim AVES2 embeddings, frozen) across 8 recording providers and 4 ecotypes [@palmer2025dclde; @palmer2025dclde_data; @hagiwara2023aves]. The artifact is schema-validated and hash-frozen (`reports/corpus_freeze.json`, SHA-256 `4f5a0c37...`) before any analysis. All numbers below are recomputed from that exact artifact.

## Headline

Frozen AVES2 embeddings carry **genuine killer-whale ecotype acoustic structure**, but **pooled decodability massively overstates it because of a recording-site shortcut**. The defensible result is the site-controlled one: when recording site is held constant, ecotype is still strongly decodable, which is biological signal the hydrophone cannot explain [@stowell2022; @ghani2023].

## Leg 1 + Leg 2: ecotype decodability and the site confound (H1, H4)

| Evaluation | Metric | Value | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pooled 5-fold CV (confounded upper bound) | balanced accuracy | 0.910 (LogReg); MLP accuracy 0.974 | Impressive but site-confounded; not the headline. |
| Leave-One-Provider-Out (cross-site) | balanced accuracy | 0.231 (chance 0.250) | Pooled signal **collapses to chance** when the test site is unseen. |
| Site-decoding probe | balanced accuracy | 0.948 (chance 0.125) | Embeddings encode **which hydrophone** recorded the call. |
| Within-site ecotype (site held constant) | balanced accuracy | 0.889 / 0.973 / 0.949 / 0.909 (JASCO_VFPA, JASCO_VFPA_ONC, ONC, SIO; all p = 0.005) | **Genuine biological signal** that survives site control. |
| Cross-site transfer (OKW vs SRKW) | balanced accuracy | 0.529 (chance 0.500) | Near chance: the within-site boundary does **not** generalise across sites. |

Per-class cross-site recall is SRKW 0.481, TKW 0.412, OKW 0.031, SAR 0.000. SAR is recorded at a single provider (UAF_NGOS), so cross-site SAR recall is structurally zero: pooled "SAR decodability" is pure site signal. Per-provider leave-one-provider-out balanced accuracy is highly variable (0.081-0.746 across the 8 held-out providers; mean about 0.42), which is itself the signature of a site-entangled representation rather than a stable cross-site decoder.

The honest conclusion is not "AVES2 decodes orca ecotype at 0.97"; it is that the 0.97 is largely hydrophone recognition, and the real biological signal is **local**: ecotype is strongly separable within any fixed site (0.89-0.97), but a site-specific offset blocks cross-site generalisation (transfer 0.529, LOPO 0.231). The embedding captures ecotype-discriminating acoustic features and a large site-specific shift at the same time.

## H2: unsupervised structure

PCA + HDBSCAN over all points gives ARI 0.043 versus ecotype (permutation p = 0.002) and ARI 0.028 versus provider. Structure is statistically above the shuffled-label null but small in absolute terms and sensitive to the reduction dimension and noise fraction (about 50% of points are unclustered at the best setting). UMAP-based reductions produce visually stronger clusters [@mcinnes2018umap; @mcinnes2017hdbscan; @sainburg2020], but UMAP is itself a non-linear embedding that can manufacture apparent structure, so the conservative PCA number is reported as the headline and the UMAP view is treated as illustration only.

## Leg 3: encounter sequence structure (H3)

Embeddings were quantised into a balanced 40-token call-type vocabulary (entropy 5.24 of 5.32 bits; no degenerate dominant token). A held-out masked language model over 702 per-source-file encounter sequences reaches loss 1.809, far below the unigram baseline of 3.486: **call co-occurrence within encounters is strongly non-random**. The masked-LM *order* test is null (real-order 1.809 vs shuffled 1.753, p = 0.77), but on ~700 encounters it is underpowered. A better-powered first-order Markov test on the same data is positive: **adjacent-call mutual information is 1.91 bits versus an order-shuffle null of 1.65 bits (p < 0.001)**, remaining **1.09 bits after removing the 40% self-transition (repetition) mass**, and a held-out bigram beats a unigram by **1.74 bits/token**. Because the shuffle preserves each encounter's call composition and only destroys order, recording-site composition cannot inflate this. The honest reading: **call streams carry non-random first-order sequential structure beyond repetition** - a prerequisite for combinatorial coding, not evidence of meaning. Both tests are reported (`scripts/run_sequence_structure.py`, `docs/decoding_program.md` Section 5c). Re-running the identical first-order test over the **validated catalogue call types** (site held constant) is also positive in both resident populations — NRKW N-calls 2.85 bits vs a 1.71-bit order-shuffle null, SRKW S-calls 1.29 vs 0.78 bits, both p = 1e-3 — which removes the unvalidated-token caveat (`scripts/run_calltype_sequence.py`, Section 5e).

## Call type: validated units recoverable and site-transferable

The DCLDE per-provider annotation files retain catalogue `call_type` codes (Ford/Filatova) that the combined release drops in favour of ecotype [@ford1989; @filatova2015]. Recovering and normalising them (`scripts/build_calltype_manifest.py`) gives a manifest of catalogue-labelled detections; the **full catalogue (8,552 detections) was encoded with AVES2** from the public DCLDE audio (`notebooks/calltype_encode_colab.ipynb`), covering both resident populations. Two confound-controlled designs follow. **Within a single provider (site held constant), a linear probe discriminates 14 SRKW S-call types at VFPA at balanced accuracy 0.709** (n=1,489; chance 0.071, majority 0.278; macro-F1 0.706; MLP 0.706) and **18 NRKW N-call types at DFO_CRP at balanced accuracy 0.968** (n=5,009; chance 0.056, majority 0.390; macro-F1 0.966; MLP 0.963), both with a 200-fold permutation null and **p ~= 0.005**. **Trained at VFPA and tested at the independent SMRU site, it reaches 0.636 balanced accuracy on the 5 shared S-call types** (chance 0.20; 0.830 on the 4 unambiguous types after dropping the merged S16/S17 label). This is the cross-site control the ecotype boundary failed (0.529): **stereotyped call-type identity is a site-independent acoustic category in both resident populations**, and residual within-site confusions fall between catalogue-adjacent types (S16/S17). It also explains the unsupervised discovery failure (below): the categories are real but too fine for unsupervised recovery from site-confounded embeddings. This is call-type discrimination, **not** evidence of meaning (labels correlate with pod/matriline). See `docs/decoding_program.md` Section 5d.

## H5: behavioural-context decode from communication (DTAG)

To test whether a whale's *communicative* calls carry information about its concurrent behaviour, we re-analyse open animal-borne suction-cup DTAG deployments on fish-eating killer whales in the Salish Sea [@holt2024masking_data; @tennessen2019; @holt2024masking]. Tag audio is losslessly decoded, communicative-call clips are cut by an energy detector in the 0.5-10 kHz pulsed-call band (impulsive clicks and >12 kHz echolocation rejected), and each dive is labelled from the tag's *movement* channel only (calibrated 50 Hz depth and acceleration). The binary label is **foraging** (depth >= 30 m or a jerk-derived prey-capture spike) vs **non-foraging**; the three-state label adds a split of the non-foraging time into **travelling** vs **resting** at the per-individual median of Overall Dynamic Body Acceleration (ODBA) [@wilson2006]. No acoustic variable enters any label, so context is independent of the call stream it is decoded from.

| Evaluation | Metric | Value | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Binary leave-individual-out decode (22 whales, 10,834 calls) | balanced accuracy | **0.770** (null 0.499 +/- 0.007, p = 0.005) | Communicative calls carry decodable information about concurrent behavioural state. |
| Three-way decode: foraging/travelling/resting (20 whales, 9,723 calls) | balanced accuracy | **0.577** (chance 0.333; null 0.355 +/- 0.008, p = 0.005) | Communication tracks **more than one** behavioural context; foraging recall 0.785, travelling 0.517, resting 0.429. |
| Call-type × context selectivity (6 data-driven types) | Cramér's V | **0.40** (within-individual null 0.08, p < 0.001) | **Specific call types in specific contexts** (production criterion); every cluster context-enriched (foraging ×2.3, travelling ×1.5, resting ×1.5). |
| Controls (binary contrast) | balanced accuracy | rate **0.536**, loudness **0.577**, low-level **0.723**, drop-25%-clicklike **0.749** vs embeddings 0.770 | Signal is call *structure*, not rate, loudness, or biosonar. |

Because every test whale is unseen in training and the label never sees the audio, the above-chance decode cannot be produced by recognising the individual, the tag, the recording site, or the acoustic energy used to define the label. This is the segment-level, identity-controlled behavioural link that the archival ecotype and call-type analyses cannot supply, and it now spans **more than one** behavioural context with **call-type-level context-specificity** — the production criterion for context-specific communication [@ford1989; @foote2008]. **It is context-specific *production* of communicative calls, not evidence of referential meaning and not evidence that receivers interpret the calls differently by context (the perception side, out of reach of an archive).** Caveats: the context labels are movement-only proxies (foraging is a dive/prey-capture signature; travelling/resting is an ODBA activity dichotomy, not a named ethogram state); the call-type clusters are soft, data-driven partitions of the on-animal tag domain, not catalogue types; echolocation peak energy is absent at the 16 kHz analysis band and the click-likeness control confirms the result is not click-driven; on-tag call-to-individual attribution is imperfect; and absolute call times assume contiguous tag audio (`reports/context_decode_summary.json`, `reports/context3_decode_summary.json`, `reports/calltype_context_selectivity.json`, `reports/context_controls_summary.json`; `figures/context_decode.png`, `figures/context3_decode.png`, `figures/calltype_context.png`, `figures/context_controls.png`; see `docs/decoding_program.md` Rung 2).

## H6: receiver response to broadcast conspecific calls (perception side)

The analyses above concern call *production*. The perception side — whether a receiver acts on a broadcast call — requires a playback experiment, which an archive cannot supply, so we re-analyse a published conspecific playback on wild killer whales [@filatova2011playback]. Recomputing the differential-response statistic from the transcribed per-trial table (`scripts/run_playback_response_stats.py`), whales replied vocally to **every same-pod playback and none of the different-pod playbacks** (8/8 vs 0/6 across all trials; **6/6 vs 0/6 after the pseudoreplication control, Fisher exact p = 0.002**), often matching the played call type [@miller2004repertoires]. The animals were silent for >= 30 min before each trial, so this is a first-exposure response, not a conditioned cue (the no-learning sub-criterion). Encoding the public FEROP call catalogue [@russianorca_catalogue] with the same frozen AVES2 model, leave-one-out 1-NN call-type purity is **0.439** across 19 Kamchatka call types vs a 0.050 label-shuffle null (**p = 1e-3**): the encoder represents the dialect space that drives the response. The criterion is corroborated across independent broadcast-response datasets — avoidance of broadcast pilot-whale sound against matched controls [@selbmann2026aversive] and orca-call structure driving receiver heading change [@bowers2018] — plus natural vocal matching [@miller2004repertoires] (roll-up in `reports/broadcast_response_criterion.json`). **This meets the response criterion (a measurable, non-invasive response of naive animals to a broadcast endogenous signal); it is not evidence of referential meaning, the behavioural experiment is prior published work re-analysed here, and the response tracks dialect membership, not call content** (see `docs/decoding_program.md` Section 5g).

## Catalogue context specialization (multi-context, named-unit level)

Beyond the movement-defined DTAG contexts, the validated catalogue call types are differentially used across functionally distinct contexts. Cross-referencing the recovered NRKW/SRKW types against the published field ethograms (`scripts/run_calltype_context_specialization.py`; context labels from the literature, not embeddings, so non-circular), **72% (13/18) of catalogue types are single-context specialists** — foraging-specialists (N4, N9) versus socializing-specialists (the multi-pod two-voiced and pod-identity calls) [@ford1989; @foote2008] (`reports/calltype_context_specialization_summary.json`, Section 5h). Broadening the axis beyond the binary foraging-vs-social contrast (`scripts/run_calltype_multicontext.py`), the same 16 named units are documented across **six** functionally distinct behavioural contexts — foraging (8 types), travelling (5), socializing (4), multi-pod aggregation (3), resting (1), greeting/excitement (1); specialization index 0.62 — so the "more than one context" criterion holds well beyond the movement-state axis at the named-unit level [@ford1989; @foote2008; @riesch2008; @yurk2002] (`reports/calltype_multicontext_summary.json`, Section 5k). The chi-square for non-uniform spread across contexts is suggestive only (p = 0.086, small n); the inferential weight on the production side is carried by the DTAG H5 decode, which this literature-grounded map complements rather than restates. Together this evidences context-specific production over functionally distinct contexts, not a single movement axis.

## H7: compositional structure beyond first order

Sections on sequence structure (H3, Rung 4) established *first-order* structure. The combinatorial-coding question the comparative literature asks — sperm-whale codas [@sharma2024], bonobo/chimpanzee compositionality [@berthet2025bonobo; @crockford2025] — is whether structure goes *beyond* first order. Testing the validated catalogue call types against **first-order Markov surrogates** that preserve unigram and bigram statistics (`scripts/run_calltype_compositionality.py`), **SRKW S-calls are positive**: the 2-back call reduces conditional entropy by 0.645 bits versus a 0.373-bit surrogate null (p ≈ 1e-3 global, p ≈ 2e-3 per-recording), a held-out trigram beats a bigram by 0.09 bits/token, and the top over-represented 3-call motif is **S01→S04→S01** (observed 13 vs 1.87 expected, z = 7.95). **NRKW N-calls are null** (0.008 bits, p = 1.0) despite heavy bouting. The honest reading: at least one resident population shows the stronger, beyond-first-order combinatorial prerequisite with recurring candidate phrases, the other does not on the public data — and this remains a statement about call *ordering*, not semantic compositionality (`reports/calltype_compositionality_summary.json`, `figures/calltype_compositionality.png`; Section 5i).

## H8: a label-free site-invariance transform (methods)

H4 showed the ecotype signal is real but local. As a methods contribution, a **label-free** transform (StandardScaler + PCA + a site-nuisance subspace projection fit on training providers only, with the held-out provider's ecotype labels never used; `scripts/run_site_invariance.py`) recovers part of the site-invariant ecotype structure: leave-one-provider-out 4-way ecotype rises from 0.402 to 0.445 (vs a 0.234 permutation null, p ≈ 0.005), cross-site SRKW-vs-TKW transfer improves 0.597 → 0.625, and within-site ecotype is preserved (0.883 → 0.876 after removing the 8 nuisance dimensions). The absolute cross-site number stays modest because ecotype and recording site are structurally confounded in this corpus (SAR is single-provider) — a ceiling reported rather than hidden [@stowell2022; @ghani2023] (`reports/site_invariance_summary.json`, `figures/site_invariance.png`; Section 5j). Not a claim of meaning.

## Legacy context heads: exploratory only

The earlier Wellard/Dryad Type C context heads inherit whole-encounter, often multi-label observer behaviour down to every fixed segment and duplicate one segment across contexts; they are weak recording-level associations, not segment-level behaviour [@wellard2020; @wellard2020_appendix2]. The "response proxy" is a within-encounter vocal-continuation timing statistic, not a measured response to a broadcast signal. None of these legacy heads is a headline claim; they are retained as scaffolding for a future playback collaboration only and are superseded for behavioural-context evidence by the DTAG H5 decode above.

## Bottom line

The pipeline is operational and yields a clean, honest, confound-aware result: AVES2 embeddings encode real killer-whale ecotype acoustic structure (recoverable only under site-controlled evaluation) and a catalogue-validated call-type repertoire that, unlike ecotype, transfers across independent recording sites (**0.636 cross-site balanced accuracy over the 5 shared SRKW S-call types; 0.830 over the 4 unambiguous types**), with non-random call co-occurrence and non-random first-order call-sequence structure over both k-means tokens and the validated catalogue call types (adjacent-call mutual information above an order-shuffle null, beyond repetition), but no syntax or meaning claim. On an independent DTAG archive, communicative calls carry decodable information about the caller's movement-defined behavioural context with the individual held out — foraging vs. non-foraging at 0.770 and a three-way foraging/travelling/resting decode at 0.577 (chance 0.333), with specific call types produced in specific contexts (Cramér's V = 0.40) and rate/loudness/echolocation controls ruling out the trivial explanations — context-specific *production* across more than one behavioural context. On the perception side, a re-analysis of a published conspecific playback shows wild killer whales respond selectively to broadcast calls (same-pod vs different-pod, 6/6 vs 0/6, p = 0.002), naive animals, corroborated across independent datasets. This is a defensible non-invasive result [@palmer2025dclde; @ford1989; @filatova2015; @holt2024masking_data; @filatova2011playback]. It does **not** demonstrate referential or semantic meaning, nor that the receiver's response is governed by call *content* rather than dialect membership; closing that step requires a content-controlled playback this archival corpus cannot provide.
