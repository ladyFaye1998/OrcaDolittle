# Site-Controlled Evaluation of Killer-Whale Ecotype and Call-Type Structure in Frozen Audio-Model Embeddings

**Author:** Danielle Lesin, Georgia Institute of Technology, College of Computing.

## Abstract

Passive bioacoustic decoding can mistake recording site for biology. We present a bounded, non-invasive killer-whale (*Orcinus orca*) analysis: one primary frozen audio encoder (AVES2), public data, provider-aware validation, permutation/null controls, and fixed claim boundaries.

Criterion 1, non-invasive decoding: 27,934 public DCLDE 2026 call segments show pooled ecotype decoding is site-inflated (0.910 balanced accuracy drops to 0.231 leave-one-provider-out; provider decodes at 0.948), but ecotype remains decodable within fixed sites (0.889-0.973). Catalogue call types are recoverable within site in both resident populations (0.709 and 0.968) and transfer across independent sites (0.636; 0.830 on unambiguous shared types).

Criterion 2, more than one context: independent DTAG recordings show communicative calls predict movement-defined behavioural context with the individual held out: foraging/non-foraging 0.770 and foraging/travelling/resting 0.577 (chance 0.333), with call-type x context selectivity and rate/loudness/echolocation controls. Published ethograms map named call types across six contexts.

Criterion 3, response to broadcast signal: re-analysis of a published conspecific playback shows wild killer whales reply to same-pod but not different-pod broadcasts (6/6 vs 0/6, Fisher p=0.002), and AVES2 recovers the dialect call types driving this response.

The work claims structure, context association, and a dialect-selective receiver response, not meaning. The remaining gap is a content-controlled field playback.

## 1. Introduction

Killer whales produce stereotyped, group-distinctive pulsed calls whose repertoires vary by ecotype and matriline [@ford1989; @foote2008; @filatova2015; @yurk2002]. Recent self-supervised audio models such as AVES2 [@hagiwara2023aves; @chen2022beats] and comparative encoders [@robinson2024naturelm; @hamer2025perch] make it tempting to treat a frozen embedding plus a linear probe as evidence that a model "understands" a species' calls. In passive-acoustic monitoring this inference is hazardous: the label of interest (here, ecotype) is frequently collinear with the recording device, deployment, and site, so a probe can score highly by recognising the hydrophone rather than the whale. This site/device shortcut is well documented in bioacoustic deep learning [@stowell2022; @ghani2023], and the standard remedy is grouped, leave-one-site-out evaluation.

We ask a deliberately narrow, auditable question: how much of the apparent ecotype decodability in frozen AVES2 embeddings is biological, and how much is recording site? We answer it with public data only, frozen weights, explicit null baselines, and a hash-frozen artifact, in the conservative-claims spirit appropriate to interspecies-communication research [@kershenbaum2024whyanimalstalk]. We do not attempt to demonstrate meaning or two-way communication; the 2025 work that established context-specific dolphin signals required decades of field data and in-situ playbacks [@sayigh2025nsw], which an archival corpus cannot supply directly. We instead address the response criterion by re-analysing a published conspecific playback (Section 4).

**Contribution.** Our contribution is methodological, not a new architecture: we treat the foundation-model encoder as fixed and build the rigour around it. We package (i) a *confound-controlled evaluation protocol* for bioacoustic foundation-model embeddings — leave-one-provider-out site isolation, within-site discrimination, and cross-site transfer — that separates biological signal from the recording-site shortcut that inflates naive probes; (ii) a *causal attribution and negative-control battery* — per-dimension permutation importance, a top-*k* vs random-*k* knock-out, and structure-matched-noise, feature-shuffle, and label-permutation nulls — that distinguishes joint embedding structure from a probe artifact; and (iii) an *embedding analysis of the dialect space that drives a published playback response*, linking broadcast-call representations to a measured receiver response. One frozen encoder runs unchanged across ecotype, call-type, sequence, behavioural-context, and playback-response analyses, so every result is attributable to the representation under a single auditable protocol rather than benchmark-chasing with a bespoke network.

## 2. Data

DCLDE 2026 is a public killer-whale acoustic corpus distributed via NOAA/NCEI and a Google Cloud Storage mirror [@palmer2025dclde; @palmer2025dclde_data]. We use 27,934 annotated call-level segments, each encoded once and stored with its source file, recording provider, ecotype label, and time bounds. The corpus spans eight providers (JASCO_VFPA, JASCO_VFPA_ONC, ONC, OrcaSound, SIMRES, SIO, SMRUConsulting, UAF_NGOS) [@orcasound] and four ecotypes: Southern Resident (SRKW, 14,240), an aggregated Salish/archival resident group (SAR, 8,078), transient/Bigg's (TKW, 4,121), and offshore (OKW, 1,495). Raw audio is excluded from version control; the analysis consumes a compact embedding artifact with row-aligned metadata, schema-validated and hash-frozen (`reports/corpus_freeze.json`, SHA-256 `4f5a0c37...`) before any model is fit. A label audit (`reports/label_audit.json`) confirms the corpus carries ecotype and provenance labels but no call-type catalogue, and that SAR is recorded at a single provider.

## 3. Methods

**Encoder.** Each segment is encoded with frozen AVES2 to a 768-dimensional vector by mean-pooling frame-level features [@hagiwara2023aves; @chen2022beats]. Weights are never fine-tuned, so downstream results are attributable to the representation, not to task-specific training.

**H1 - supervised probes.** We report three evaluations of increasing strictness: (i) pooled stratified 5-fold cross-validation with logistic-regression and multilayer-perceptron probes (the site-confounded upper bound); (ii) leave-one-provider-out (LOPO) cross-validation, where every test provider is unseen in training, with per-class held-out recall; and (iii) a label-shuffle permutation test. Class imbalance is handled with balanced accuracy and macro-F1 [@stowell2022; @ghani2023].

**H4 - confound isolation.** We (i) decode provider directly from embeddings to size the confound, (ii) classify ecotype *within* each provider that recorded two or more ecotypes (site held constant), with a per-provider label-permutation null, and (iii) measure cross-site transfer by training and testing on disjoint providers.

**Call-type classification.** The DCLDE combined release standardises killer-whale labels to ecotype, but the original per-provider annotation files retain a populated `call_type` column (Ford/Filatova catalogue codes) [@ford1989; @filatova2015]. We recover and normalise these into a call-type manifest and encode the full catalogue (8,552 labelled detections) directly from the public DCLDE audio with the same frozen AVES2 model, covering both resident populations. We then run two site-controlled designs: (i) within-provider multi-type classification with site held constant (14 SRKW S-call types at VFPA; 18 NRKW N-call types at DFO_CRP) using a linear probe and an MLP head, scored against a majority baseline and a 200-fold label-permutation null, and (ii) cross-provider transfer, training at VFPA and testing at SMRU on the SRKW call types shared by both.

**H2 - unsupervised structure.** Embeddings are reduced with PCA and clustered with HDBSCAN [@mcinnes2017hdbscan]; cluster-versus-ecotype and cluster-versus-provider agreement (ARI, NMI) are compared against a shuffled-label null over all points [@sainburg2020; @mcinnes2018umap].

**H3 - sequence structure.** Embeddings are quantised by PCA + balanced k-means into a 40-token call vocabulary; token entropy confirms a non-degenerate alphabet. We test first-order dependence directly with the mutual information between adjacent calls against a within-encounter order-shuffle null (which fixes each encounter's call composition and destroys only order) and an add-alpha bigram-vs-unigram held-out comparison, in the spirit of combinatorial-structure tests on cetacean calls [@sharma2024]. A 6-layer masked-language model provides a complementary held-out order test [@vaswani2017attention; @devlin2019bert]; on ~700 encounters it is underpowered and returns a null, so the better-powered Markov test is the headline and both are reported.

**Attribution and negative controls.** To characterise where the biological signal lives in the frozen representation and to rule out a probe artifact, we dissect the cleanest within-site decode (SRKW vs TKW within JASCO_VFPA): per-dimension permutation importance, a top-k vs random-k causal knock-out, and a PCA-dimensionality curve, plus three negative controls that must fall to chance if the signal depends on joint embedding structure (structure-matched Gaussian noise, a per-dimension feature shuffle, and the label-permutation null).

**H5 - behavioural-context decode from communication (DTAG).** To test whether a whale's *communicative* calls carry information about its concurrent behaviour, we re-analyse open suction-cup DTAG deployments on fish-eating killer whales in the Salish Sea [@holt2024masking_data; @tennessen2019; @holt2024masking]. Tag audio (`.dtg`, DTAG-2 archive format) is decoded losslessly to WAV with the reference DTAG-2 extractor (FFSRD; Mark Johnson) and segmented into communicative-call clips by an energy detector in the 0.5-10 kHz pulsed-call band, rejecting impulsive broadband clicks (by duration) and segments dominated by >12 kHz energy (echolocation), so the decoder sees communication rather than sonar. Behavioural context is defined from the tag's *movement* channel only: from the calibrated 50 Hz depth and acceleration records (`prh50.mat`), a dive (depth > 5 m for >= 10 s) is labelled *foraging* if it reaches >= 30 m or contains a jerk-derived prey-capture spike, otherwise *non-foraging*, following the kinematic foraging signature for this population [@tennessen2019; @holt2024masking]. No acoustic variable enters the label, so the context is independent of the call stream we decode it from. Each clip is encoded with the same frozen AVES2 model and inherits the movement-defined context of the dive containing its absolute time. Context is decoded from the call embeddings with a logistic-regression probe and an MLP head under **leave-individual-out** cross-validation (each tagged whale held out in turn), scored against a label-permutation null; holding the individual out prevents a model from scoring by recognising the individual, the tag, or the site rather than the behaviour.

We then add three checks. First, to test communication in *more than one* context, we resolve a three-way label — *foraging* (as above) versus *travelling* and *resting*, the latter two separated within each deployment at the median of Overall Dynamic Body Acceleration (ODBA) derived from the calibrated acceleration record [@wilson2006]; the split is per-individual and uses no acoustic variable, so neither between-tag gain differences nor sound can stand in for behaviour. Second, to test the production criterion for context-specific communication — that *specific* call types are produced in *specific* contexts [@ford1989; @foote2008] — we cluster the call embeddings into data-driven call types and test the call-type-by-context association (Cramer's V) against a null that shuffles context labels *within each individual*, preserving every whale's own context marginal so the association cannot arise from individuals differing in repertoire or activity budget. Third, to exclude trivial explanations, we re-decode context from (i) local call rate alone, (ii) loudness (log-RMS) alone, (iii) low-level acoustic scalars, and (iv) the embeddings after discarding the most click-like clips; because the tag audio is analysed at 16 kHz, echolocation peak energy (tens of kHz) is absent by construction, so the decoder operates in the communicative band.

**H6 - receiver response to broadcast conspecific calls (perception side).** To address the perception side, which no archive can supply, we re-analyse a published conspecific playback experiment on wild killer whales [@filatova2011playback]. We transcribe its per-trial outcomes (stimulus class, vocal response, response call types) into a small table and recompute the same-pod vs different-pod response contingency with a two-sided Fisher exact test, both on all trials and after the authors' pseudoreplication control. Separately, to test whether the frozen encoder represents the dialect signal space underlying the response contrast, we encode the public FEROP Kamchatka call catalogue [@russianorca_catalogue] with the same frozen AVES2 model and measure leave-one-out 1-NN call-type purity against a label-permutation null. The playback experiment itself is prior work; our contribution is the reproducible statistic and the embedding analysis.

All scripts are deterministic under a fixed seed; commands and the software snapshot are in the repository.

## 4. Results

**Pooled decodability is high but site-confounded.** Pooled 5-fold cross-validation reaches 0.910 balanced accuracy (logistic regression) and 0.974 accuracy (multilayer perceptron), with a label-permutation p of 5e-3. Under leave-one-provider-out, however, balanced accuracy falls to 0.231 (macro-F1 0.213) against a chance level of 0.250. Per-class cross-site recall is SRKW 0.481, TKW 0.412, OKW 0.031, and SAR 0.000; because SAR is recorded at one provider, it is structurally unclassifiable cross-site, and its pooled contribution is pure site signal.

**The site signal is large; within-site biological structure remains.** Provider is decodable from the embeddings at 0.948 balanced accuracy (chance 0.125), confirming a large confound. Within-site ecotype discrimination, where the hydrophone is fixed, remains above chance: SRKW versus TKW reaches 0.889 (JASCO_VFPA, n = 6,962) and 0.973 (JASCO_VFPA_ONC, n = 1,511); three-way discrimination reaches 0.949 among OKW, SRKW, and TKW (ONC, n = 803), 0.909 among OKW, SRKW, and TKW (SIO, n = 2,637), and 0.969 among OKW, SAR, and TKW (UAF_NGOS, n = 10,113), all with permutation p = 5e-3. Cross-site transfer, by contrast, is near chance: training on a subset of providers and testing on disjoint providers gives 0.529 balanced accuracy (chance 0.500; OKW vs SRKW). The embedding separates ecotypes within the five fixed-site tests, but a site-specific offset blocks the decision boundary from generalising across sites, which is why leave-one-provider-out collapses.

**Validated call types are recoverable in both resident populations and, unlike ecotype, transfer across sites.** Encoding the full catalogue (8,552 labelled detections), frozen AVES2 vectors discriminate call types within a single provider above chance in both resident populations: 14 Southern-Resident S-call types at VFPA (n = 1,489) at 0.709 balanced accuracy (chance 0.071, majority 0.278, macro-F1 0.706; MLP 0.706), and 18 Northern-Resident N-call types at DFO_CRP (n = 5,009) at 0.968 balanced accuracy (chance 0.056, majority 0.390, macro-F1 0.966; MLP 0.963), each with a 200-fold permutation null at chance (p ~= 0.005). In the cross-site test, a Southern-Resident classifier trained at VFPA and tested at the independent SMRU site reaches 0.636 balanced accuracy (macro-F1 0.607) on the five shared S-call types (chance 0.20), rising to 0.830 on the four unambiguous types after the merged S16/S17 label is dropped. This is the cross-site control the ecotype boundary failed (0.529): stereotyped call-type identity is more site-transferable than broad ecotype in this corpus, and residual within-site confusions fall between catalogue-adjacent labels (S16, S16/S17, S17).

**Unsupervised structure is weak but above null.** PCA + HDBSCAN yields ARI 0.043 versus ecotype (permutation p = 2e-3) and ARI 0.028 versus provider, above the shuffled-label null but small in absolute value and sensitive to the reduction dimension and to a roughly 50% noise fraction.

**Call streams carry non-random sequential structure.** The 40-token vocabulary is balanced (entropy 5.24 of a maximum 5.32 bits). Across 702 encounters and 27,112 adjacent call pairs, the mutual information between consecutive calls is 1.909 bits, above an order-shuffle null of 1.652 +/- 0.006 bits (permutation p < 0.001); because the shuffle preserves each encounter's call composition and only destroys order, this excess is attributable to sequence rather than to site. The structure is not merely repetition: the self-transition rate is 0.40, yet off-diagonal mutual information is still 1.088 bits. Out of sample, an add-alpha bigram model beats a unigram by 1.741 bits/token. A complementary masked-language model returns a null on the same data (real-order loss 1.809 vs shuffled 1.753, p = 0.77); on ~700 encounters the first-order Markov test is the better-powered instrument, and we report both. This is the production-side prerequisite for combinatorial coding, not evidence of meaning. Separately, unsupervised within-SRKW clustering does not recover a repertoire (clusters 93% single-provider, 82% noise); the categories are too fine to fall out of site-confounded embeddings, which is why the supervised call-type result above relies on catalogue labels [@ford1989; @filatova2015; @wellard2020_data].

**The sequential structure holds over validated call types, not just quantised tokens.** Re-running the identical first-order test on the recovered catalogue call types (per-recording sequences, recording site held constant, within-recording order-shuffle null) is positive in both resident populations. For Northern-Resident N-calls (31 catalogue types, 5,273 calls at DFO_CRP) the adjacent-pair mutual information is 2.85 bits against an order-shuffle null of 1.71 bits (p = 1e-3); the streams are heavily bouted (self-transition rate 0.92) but retain 1.60 bits of transition structure after removing repetition, and a held-out bigram beats a unigram by 2.73 bits/token. For Southern-Resident S-calls (19 catalogue types, 1,553 calls at VFPA) the mutual information is 1.29 bits against a null of 0.78 bits (p = 1e-3), with a self-transition rate of 0.52 and a held-out bigram gain of 0.31 bits/token. This repeats the sequence test over expert catalogue call types, with site held constant, in both populations [@ford1989; @filatova2015; @sharma2024]. It remains a statement about call ordering, not meaning.

**Communicative calls carry decodable information about the caller's behavioural context, with the individual held out.** On an independent archive of animal-borne DTAG recordings of Salish-Sea killer whales [@holt2024masking_data], we losslessly decode the tag audio, cut 10,972 communicative-call clips across 23 deployments, and label each call with the movement-only context (foraging vs non-foraging) of the dive containing it; 22 of the 23 tagged whales contribute both contexts (one tag logged no qualifying foraging dive). Under leave-individual-out cross-validation across all 22 individuals (10,834 calls; 2,692 foraging, 8,142 non-foraging), a linear probe on the frozen AVES2 call embeddings predicts the movement-defined context at 0.770 balanced accuracy, against a label-permutation null of 0.499 +/- 0.007 (permutation p = 5e-3). Because every test whale is unseen in training, and the label is derived solely from the tag's depth and acceleration channels rather than from sound, this above-chance decode cannot be produced by recognising the individual, the tag, or the recording site, nor by the acoustic energy used to define the label: the communicative calls themselves carry information about what the animal is doing [@tennessen2019; @holt2024masking]. This is an association between communicative-call structure and concurrent behavioural state, not a claim of referential meaning, but it is the segment-level, identity-controlled behavioural link that the archival ecotype and call-type analyses above could not supply.

**The link spans more than one context, is call-type-specific, and is not a rate, loudness, or echolocation artefact.** Resolving three movement-defined contexts, the same leave-individual-out decode reaches 0.577 balanced accuracy across the 20 whales that visit all three (9,723 calls; chance 0.333; within-individual permutation null 0.355 +/- 0.008, p = 5e-3), with per-context recall highest for foraging (0.785) and lower but above chance for travelling (0.517) and resting (0.429) - foraging is the most acoustically distinct context, and the softer travelling/resting boundary is expected of an activity-based split. The association is call-type-specific: clustering the calls into data-driven types gives a call-type-by-context table with Cramer's V = 0.40 against a within-individual shuffle null of 0.079 +/- 0.006 (p < 1e-3), and every recovered type is over-represented in a particular context (e.g. one type x2.3 and two others x1.5-1.9 in foraging, one x1.5 in travelling, one x1.5 in resting), the production-side signature of context-specific calling [@ford1989; @foote2008]. Control decodes confirm the signal is in call *structure*: local call rate alone decodes the binary contrast at only 0.536 and loudness alone at 0.577, against the embeddings' 0.770; coarse acoustic scalars (spectral shape and duration) reach 0.723; and removing the 25% most click-like clips leaves the decode at 0.749 (the click-like quartile alone gives 0.751). The result is therefore neither "more calls" in one context, nor "louder calls", nor residual biosonar [@wilson2006].

**The biological signal is distributed and is not a probe artifact.** Attribution on the within-site SRKW/TKW decode (5-fold balanced accuracy 0.979) shows the signal is multi-dimensional and redundant rather than a single suspicious channel: one AVES2 dimension decodes at chance (0.48), a low-rank PCA projection already recovers most of the signal (~10 components reach 0.80, 100 reach 0.91), and ablating the top-k permutation-important dimensions does not collapse the decode (it stays 0.93-0.97 through k=200, as does ablating k random dimensions). Three negative controls support the interpretation that the decode uses joint structure rather than a marginal artifact or a leak: structure-matched Gaussian noise decodes at 0.536, a per-dimension feature shuffle at 0.505, and the label-permutation null at 0.503 (p = 8e-3), all near the 0.500 chance level against the embeddings' 0.979.

**Receivers respond selectively to broadcast conspecific calls (perception side).** The analyses above are about call *production*; the perception side - whether a receiver acts on a broadcast call - requires a playback experiment, which an archive cannot supply. We therefore re-analyse a published conspecific playback experiment on wild killer whales [@filatova2011playback], in which 2-min sequences of same-pod versus different-pod discrete calls were broadcast to free-ranging Kamchatkan resident whales and the per-trial response scored. Recomputing the differential-response statistic from the transcribed per-trial table, whales replied vocally to every same-pod playback and to none of the different-pod playbacks (8/8 vs 0/6 across all trials; 6/6 vs 0/6 after the authors' pseudoreplication control), a significant difference (Fisher exact two-sided p = 0.002), and when they replied they often matched the played call type [@miller2004repertoires]. The animals were silent for at least 30 min before each trial, so this is a response to a first-exposure broadcast, not an associatively conditioned cue. To show that the frozen encoder represents the signal space driving this selectivity, we encode the public FEROP call catalogue [@russianorca_catalogue] with the same frozen AVES2 model: leave-one-out 1-NN call-type purity is 0.439 across 19 Kamchatka call types against a label-shuffle null of 0.050 +/- 0.033 (p = 1e-3; proportional-chance 0.067), so the embeddings recover the dialect call types. The behavioural experiment is prior published work; our contribution is the reproducible statistic and the embedding model, and the response tracks dialect membership rather than call content.

**Additional response and catalogue-level context evidence.** The response result is supported by independent studies: an independent open dataset shows killer whales avoid broadcast pilot-whale sound against matched noise/upsweep controls (Stimulus p = 0.02) [@selbmann2026aversive], a second shows that the structure of broadcast killer-whale calls drives selective receiver heading changes [@bowers2018], and natural exchanges show receivers match a preceding caller's type above chance [@miller2004repertoires] (roll-up in `reports/broadcast_response_criterion.json`). On the production side, the recovered catalogue call types specialise across two functionally distinct contexts: of 18 Northern- and Southern-Resident call types with a documented foraging or social context, 72% are single-context specialists (foraging-specialists e.g. N4 and N9; socialising-specialists the multi-pod two-voiced and pod-identity calls), with the context labels taken from published ethograms rather than our embeddings (so the map is non-circular) [@ford1989; @foote2008]. Across the program, then, context-specific signalling is evidenced over *functionally distinct* contexts - foraging, travelling and resting on the production side (H5), social/affiliative use of named call types (catalogue), and an anti-predator/threat response to a broadcast stimulus [@selbmann2026aversive] - not a single movement-arousal axis.

**Second-encoder check.** Repeating the FEROP dialect-space and site-controlled call-type checks with frozen NatureLM-audio [@robinson2024naturelm] preserves the direction of the bounded result: FEROP K-type 1-NN purity is 0.366 vs 0.050 proportional chance (p = 0.000999), VFPA/SRKW and DFO-CRP/NRKW within-provider call-type balanced accuracies are 0.709 and 0.800, and VFPA -> SMRU transfer is 0.682 over five shared types (chance 0.20). This is a cross-encoder check, not a new semantic claim.

## 5. Limitations

The pooled 0.97 figure is not a biological result and we do not report it as one; the bounded estimate is the within-site number. Cross-site transfer for ecotype is bounded by the single-provider ecotype (SAR) and by absolute embedding-space shifts between sites. The call-type result covers the two resident populations (SRKW and NRKW) but not Bigg's or offshore types; the catalogue labels are detection-level and correlate with pod and matriline, so it is call-type discrimination, not evidence of meaning, and the cross-site transfer estimate rests on a modest SMRU test set (267 calls, five shared types). Unsupervised structure is weak and reduction-dependent. The earlier recording-level "response-proxy" and timing analyses retained in the repository are exploratory associations, not segment-level behaviour and not responses to broadcast signals [@wellard2020; @wellard2020_appendix2]; they remain excluded from every claim here. The behavioural-context decode (H5) is, by contrast, segment-level and individual-held-out, but carries its own boundaries: the context labels are movement-only proxies, so foraging is a dive-depth/prey-capture signature and the travelling/resting split is an ODBA activity dichotomy rather than a named ethogram state (e.g. socialising or alarm are not resolved), and the result is therefore an association between communicative-call structure and concurrent behavioural context, not evidence of referential meaning. The call-type clusters are data-driven partitions of the on-animal tag domain, not the named DCLDE catalogue types, and the cluster structure is soft (low silhouette); the call-type-by-context association uses a null that reuses the same clustering. Echolocation is addressed by the 16 kHz analysis band (which excludes click peak energy) and by the click-likeness control, but on-tag attribution of calls to the focal whale is imperfect; and absolute call times assume contiguous tag audio from the start of the movement record. Most fundamentally, the archival heads (H1-H5) are a non-invasive decoding study of call *production*; the one *response* result (H6) is a re-analysis of a published conspecific playback experiment [@filatova2011playback], not an experiment we ran, and it shows a dialect-selective response (the perception criterion) but not that the receiver's response is governed by call *content* - so it is not evidence of referential call-level meaning. The small playback sample (6 vs 6 after pseudoreplication control) is a real power limit, and the per-trial stimulus-to-response embedding model is gated on request-only session audio.

**Channel completeness.** Killer whales also communicate through non-acoustic channels — body posture, surface behaviour, and tactile contact — that audio archives do not capture. This does not undermine the results, which concern only the acoustic channel: the production analyses ask what acoustic structure exists, and the playback re-analysis measures a response to an acoustic broadcast, so an omitted visual or tactile cue cannot inflate an acoustic decode or a response to sound. It does bound interpretation — absence of an acoustic effect would not imply absence of communication, and a complete account of context-specific signalling would require synchronised multimodal observation [@esp2025raincoast].

**Arousal versus content.** A behavioural-context decode could in principle reflect a graded arousal or activity axis rather than context-specific signal content. The link is carried by *which* call types occur (call-type x context Cramer's V = 0.40, specific types in specific contexts) rather than a monotone intensity shift, and loudness alone decodes context at only 0.577 against the embeddings' 0.770, which argues against a pure-arousal account; we do not claim to have fully separated arousal from content.

**Scope of inference.** The within-site ecotype and catalogue call-type analyses test acoustic structure under provider controls, including cross-site call-type transfer and negative controls. The context result is production-side: communicative calls are associated with more than one movement-defined context with the individual held out, but the labels are movement proxies and the on-tag clusters are soft. The response result is a re-analysis of a published playback experiment (same-pod vs different-pod, p = 0.002), supported by independent broadcast-response datasets [@selbmann2026aversive; @bowers2018; @miller2004repertoires], but bounded by modest conspecific n and prior-published behavioural data. Out of scope: referential meaning, and any claim that the receiver's response is governed by call content rather than dialect membership.

## 6. Reproducibility

The repository provides exact commands, a hash-frozen artifact (`reports/corpus_freeze.json`), per-head metrics JSON and figures, a label audit, and a software snapshot. Each reported number is regenerated by `scripts/run_h1_probes.py`, `scripts/run_h4_confound.py`, `scripts/run_h2_clustering.py`, `scripts/run_h3_sequence_lm.py`, and `scripts/run_calltype_model.py` (with the call-type manifest built by `scripts/build_calltype_manifest.py`); the full-catalogue call-type encode and models are reproduced by `notebooks/calltype_encode_colab.ipynb` (`reports/calltype_model_full_summary.json`), and the validated-call-type sequence test by `scripts/run_calltype_sequence.py` (`reports/calltype_sequence_summary.json`). The behavioural-context decode (H5) is reproduced end to end from public DTAG deposits by `scripts/dtag_local_extract.py` (downloads each `.dtg`, losslessly decodes it with the reference DTAG-2 extractor, detects communicative calls, and cuts clips), `scripts/dtag_context_labels.py` (builds the movement-only foraging/non-foraging label from `prh50.mat` and reports per-individual yield in `reports/context_label_yield.json`), and `scripts/dtag_context_decode.py` (frozen-AVES2 encode, leave-individual-out decode, permutation null; `reports/context_decode_summary.json`, `figures/context_decode.png`). The multi-context strengthening is reproduced by `scripts/dtag_context_multilabel.py` (three-state foraging/travelling/resting labels from depth, jerk, and ODBA; `reports/context3_label_yield.json`), `scripts/dtag_context_multidecode.py` (three-way leave-individual-out decode and within-individual permutation null; `reports/context3_decode_summary.json`, `figures/context3_decode.png`), `scripts/dtag_calltype_context.py` (call-type clustering and call-type-by-context selectivity with a within-individual null; `reports/calltype_context_selectivity.json`, `figures/calltype_context.png`), and `scripts/dtag_context_controls.py` (call-rate, loudness, low-level-acoustic, and echolocation-leakage controls; `reports/context_controls_summary.json`, `figures/context_controls.png`); these reuse the frozen-AVES2 call embeddings, so no re-encoding is required. The representation attribution and negative-control battery are reproduced by `scripts/run_attribution_controls.py` (`reports/attribution_controls_summary.json`, `figures/attribution_controls.png`). Derived artifacts, metrics, figures, provenance tables, and reproduction notebooks are deposited on Zenodo [@orcadolittle_zenodo]. Raw audio and tag archives are downloaded from public sources and excluded from version control.

## 7. Outlook

A site-controlled decoding baseline is the credible foundation for the next scientific step: pairing these embeddings with field groups capable of in-situ playbacks to test whether specific call structures elicit measurable, context-specific responses, as demonstrated for dolphins [@sayigh2025nsw]. We release the pipeline to make that collaboration concrete and reproducible.
