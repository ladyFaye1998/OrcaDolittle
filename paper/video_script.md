# Two-minute video &mdash; locked structure, placeholder content

> **Status.** Structure aligned with the locked plan as of 2026-05-18. No footage yet because no analysis has been performed yet. Drafting begins when Stage 5 of `EXECUTION_PLAN.md` is underway and the H1 / H4 headline figures exist. Pitched at the [@kershenbaum2024whyanimalstalk] audience &mdash; intelligent non-specialist &mdash; not at the technical reviewer.

A two-minute video is two pages of double-spaced narration at most. Less is better.

## Four-beat structure

**Beat 1 &mdash; The question (15 s).** One sentence stating what is being asked. One sentence stating why the answer is hard, with one nod to [@yovel2023doctor]'s three obstacles. Camera: still title card over an orca call spectrogram drawn from [@palmer2025dclde].

**Beat 2 &mdash; The data (20 s).** A sentence on the dataset [@palmer2025dclde]: 225,000 annotated calls, 23 sites, three ecotypes. A sentence on the encoder [@robinson2024naturelm]. One quick visual of the public-data provenance, with the dataset citation on-screen.

**Beat 3 &mdash; The result (60 s).** The headline figure from head H1 + H2 on screen, animated *once*. The result stated in one sentence. The counterfactual control (permutation baseline) shown for ten seconds. The H4 playback-prediction result stated, this time with the confidence interval. Frame as: *"the same embedding-distance metric that distinguishes resident-from-transient calls quantitatively predicts the responses orcas give to playback in published experiments [@bowers2018; @cure2026; @filatova2011]."*

**Beat 4 &mdash; Limitations (25 s).** Three short sentences: what the result does *not* imply (no "meaning" claim, no Wittgenstein-boundary crossing [@wittgenstein1953]). Whose work it builds on [@ford1989; @foote2008; @filatova2015; @sharma2024]. What would be needed before any real interspecies communication claim could be made.

## What this script will *not* contain

- The phrase "Doctor Dolittle".
- The phrase "we talked to whales".
- The word "language" without an immediate qualifier.
- Any mood music dramatic enough to overwhelm the orca audio.
- Any visualisation that has not been generated by code that lives in this repository at submission time.
