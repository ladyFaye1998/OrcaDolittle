What we actually did

Six findings, every number reproducible from public data.

One frozen audio foundation model (AVES2) runs unchanged across every analysis; the contribution is the rigour around it — measuring the recording-site shortcut that fools naive probes, and reporting only what survives explicit null baselines.

The trap

Most of the “97% accuracy” was the microphone

0.948

A frozen model “decodes ecotype” at 0.91 pooled — but hold out a whole recording site and it collapses to 0.23 (chance 0.25), while the site itself decodes at 0.948. So we measured the confound instead of hiding it.

Stowell 2022 · Ghani 2023 · Palmer et al. 2025
Real biology

Ecotype is decodable once the site is held constant

0.889–0.973

Within each of five fixed recording sites the embeddings still separate ecotypes — the hydrophone never changes, so that signal has to be the whale.

Hagiwara 2023 (AVES) · Chen et al. 2022
Units that travel

Named call types transfer across sites

0.71 / 0.97

Catalogue call types are recovered within a site (14 SRKW types 0.71; 18 NRKW types 0.97) and — unlike ecotype — transfer to an independent site (0.64; 0.83 on unambiguous types): the control ecotype failed.

Ford 1989 · Filatova et al. 2015
More than one context

Calls predict what the whale is doing

0.770

On animal-borne tags, communicative calls decode movement-defined context (foraging vs not 0.77; three-way 0.58) with every individual held out, and specific call types occur in specific contexts (V=0.40). Not louder, not more frequent — we checked.

Holt, Tennessen et al. 2024 · Wilson et al. 2006
Structure

Sequence structure, and — in SRKW — beyond first order

+0.645 bits

Calls don’t come at random: one call predicts the next, and Southern-Resident sequences carry information a first-order model can’t explain (candidate phrase S01→S04→S01). A prerequisite for combinatorial coding — not meaning.

Sharma et al. 2024 · Berthet et al. 2025 · Girard-Buttoz et al. 2025
A measured response

Wild whales answer same-pod calls, ignore strangers

p = 0.002

Re-analysing a published playback: free-ranging whales replied to same-pod broadcasts (6/6) and stayed silent to different-pod ones (0/6), naive animals — and our model recovers the dialect call types that drive it (purity 0.439 vs 0.05 null). The experiment is prior work; the response tracks dialect, not content.

Filatova et al. 2011 · FEROP catalogue · Miller et al. 2004

For field scientists

Let’s run the experiment an archive cannot.

Archival recordings can characterise call structure and its confounds, and we’ve done that rigorously. What they can’t do — even in principle — is measure how a whale responds to a signal deliberately broadcast to it. That needs a controlled in-situ playback with a permitted field group. If that’s you, we’ve already built the analysis half.

What this project brings

  • A frozen-embedding pipeline for killer-whale calls with hash-frozen artifacts and per-head metrics.
  • A site-controlled evaluation protocol that separates real acoustic structure from the recording-site shortcut — so any field claim rests on defensible methods.
  • Validated catalogue call types that transfer across sites, and a behavioural-context decoder, ready to condition on your observed contexts.
  • Code that scores playback stimulus & response audio against the same frozen representation, drop-in.

What a field partner brings

  • Permitted, non-invasive in-situ playback with a study population that has individual/group ID.
  • Per-trial response measurement (movement, vocal, behavioural) with pre-exposure and matched controls.
  • The one design that closes the loop: a content-controlled, conspecific playback — same dialect, different call type — to test whether whales respond to a call’s content, not just whose voice it is.
  • Observer-grounded, segment-level behavioural context.

Run the tool yourself

Everything is open and reproducible. Clone it, point it at the public DCLDE data, and regenerate every number:

git clone https://github.com/ladyFaye1998/OrcaDolittle
cd OrcaDolittle && pip install -e ".[dev,analysis]"
python scripts/run_h4_confound.py --embeddings data/embeddings/aves2_full_labeled.npz

Where we draw the line

Honesty is the method, not the disclaimer.

✓ What we claim

  • Killer-whale call structure is decodable from frozen embeddings once the recording site is controlled.
  • Validated catalogue call types are recoverable and transfer across sites.
  • Calls carry information about more than one behavioural context, with the individual held out.
  • Call sequences are non-random, and in Southern Residents show structure beyond first order.
  • Wild whales produce a measurable, dialect-selective response to broadcast conspecific calls (by re-analysis of a published experiment).

✗ What we do not claim

  • That we can translate orca calls or that any call has a known “meaning”.
  • That the playback response is governed by call content rather than dialect membership.
  • That decoded structure equals language, syntax-with-semantics, or thought.
  • That we ran a new field experiment — the response result is a re-analysis of prior published work.
  • Two-way or interactive communication. That remains future work — the reason this page exists.
